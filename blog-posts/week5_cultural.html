Pairing and Feedback

Written by Ronald Chacon on September 23, 2014.

Pairing

What was it like to pair with others to solve challenges?

This is something I had been doing since college. In my degree, MIS, it really stressed teamwork.

Was something particularly fun or rewarding?

I find being able to solve the solution within a team is very rewarding. Also another benefit is being able to understand how other colleagues would come to a solution.

Was something frustrating and difficult for you?

Pairing remotely was a new experience and it was a bit uncomforable pairing remotely my first time. However, after the first one, it was very easy to pair.

Feedback

How did you feel when you read your feedback?

I think it was interesting. I never had someone rate me on how well I am to partner with. I really liked the system as it helped me improve over time, and I feel I have been doing so over time.

Was the feedback you received helpful to your learning?

Yes, I feel the feedback was very helpful, as it helped me correct some things I was not doing. For example, I like to get right into the problem, while others do not. With the feedback I was able to slow it down and get to know my partner.

Based on the feedback you've received, what are you going to do to improve next time you pair?

Overtime, I have been slowing down at working with others. My inittial pairing wasn't the greatest and it was because I rushed the whole pairing experience.

How was it to write feedback?

Writing feedback is relatively easy. However I feel it can be hard hard to write feedback if you don't know what to say or how to start. I know this was the case for me, as I really know didn't know how to write constructive feedback. However looking at feedbackinator, I was able to get a better grasp.

What was most difficult for you when writing feedback for your pair?

The only hard part that I have is when I think my partner doesn't have anything improve on. For example, some people I pair with are really good and programming and working on teams and pairing is a breeze.

Overall I think pairing and feedback is a great way to boost learning as it can realy help with my weak points. For example, if my partner feels I am struggling with a Ruby concept, I can focus on that while I study. Also with the feedback application, this can help a lot with working in teams at DBC and in the future as a developer.
